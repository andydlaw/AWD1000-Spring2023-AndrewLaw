I am a Paramedic with a Flight Paramedic and Tactical Paramedic Certification. I enjoy working autonomously and have the right combination of non-traditional mentoring, training in clinical procedures, leadership experience, and medical knowledge to be a Surge Division Battalion Chief at SCCAD.

I began practicing emergency medicine as a Corpsman for Marines in the Navy. I deployed multiple times to Iraq and Afghanistan as a non-commissioned officer overseeing and mentoring five to fifteen Corpsmen, maintaining the health and welfare of thirty to fifty Marines. As the Single Resource Paramedic on C-Shift at SCCAD, I managed the care of high acuity critical care transfers, developed a Clinical Practice Guideline for pre-hospital finger thoracotomy, and mentored new Single Resources and critical care paramedics. Both life experiences gave me the skills and opportunities to successfully lead small units to achieve high-stakes mission goals.

The critically ill need a strong practitioner to safely transport them from point A to point B while they are unstable, and traditional emergency medicine is not enough. I welcome the opportunity to inspire, challenge, and lead the new generation of those practitioners for our patients.
